CHAPTER 1

Getting Started — Logging In

How to open the system and sign in to your account

Opening the System

The JIFS Fleet System runs directly on the office computer — no internet connection is needed. To open it:

  1. 1
    Open the web browser Use Google Chrome or any browser on the office computer.
  2. 2
    Go to the system address Type http://localhost:5173 in the address bar at the top, then press Enter.
  3. 3
    The Login page appears You will see a form asking for your username and password.
Screenshot: Login Page
Shows the blue login form with JIFS Fleet logo, username field, password field, and "Sign In" button

Signing In

  1. 1
    Type your username Click on the "Username" box and type your username (e.g. admin).
  2. 2
    Type your password Click on the "Password" box and type your password. The characters will appear as dots for security.
  3. 3
    Click "Sign In" The system will take you straight to the Dashboard.
Your session stays active for 24 hours

You do not need to log in again each time you use the system within the same day. If you close the browser and reopen it the next day, you will need to sign in again.

Signing Out

When you are done for the day, click the red Logout button at the very bottom of the left-side menu. This keeps the system secure so other people cannot use your account.

User Roles

RoleWhat they can do
AdminFull access — add, edit, delete everything including vehicles and suppliers. Create other users.
OperatorCan record expenses, services, and parts. Cannot delete records or add vehicles/suppliers.

Dashboard — Your Home Screen

A quick overview of everything happening in your fleet

The Dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It gives you a quick summary of the entire fleet at a glance — you do not need to click through every section to know the current status.

Screenshot: Full Dashboard
Shows the 4 blue/green/red/amber stat cards at the top, quick action buttons, and the alerts panels below

Summary Cards (Top Row)

The four coloured boxes at the top give you the most important numbers:

CardWhat it shows
Active VehiclesHow many trucks are currently marked as active in the system.
SuppliersTotal number of suppliers registered.
Total ExpensesThe grand total of all money spent (debited) across all time.
This MonthTotal expenses recorded this calendar month only.

Quick Action Buttons

Three shortcut buttons appear once your vehicles and suppliers are set up:

  • Log a Transaction — Takes you directly to the Expenses page to record a new entry.
  • Print Statement — Takes you to the supplier statement for printing or reconciliation.
  • View Reports — Opens the full reports section.

Budget Warnings

If you have set a monthly spending budget for a truck and that truck has spent 80% or more of the budget this month, a warning card appears automatically. It shows a progress bar and percentage so you can see at a glance which trucks are spending too much.

Screenshot: Budget Warning Card
Shows an amber warning card with a progress bar for each truck that is near or over budget, with percentage displayed

Alerts — Upcoming Services

The purple panel shows trucks that have a scheduled service coming up within the next 30 days. Each row shows the truck plate number, what the service is, and the date it is due.

Alerts — Expiring Parts

The yellow panel shows spare parts that are expiring within 30 days. This reminds you to replace or renew them before they expire.

Alerts — Vehicle Documents

A red panel appears if any vehicle documents (like TRA sticker, road licence, or insurance) have expired or will expire within 60 days. Each row shows the truck, the document type, and how many days are left (or how many days ago it expired).

Always check the Dashboard first

Start every working day on the Dashboard. Any expired documents or overdue services will appear here as red alerts so nothing is missed.

First-Time Setup Guidance

If you have just installed the system and haven't added any trucks or suppliers yet, the Dashboard will show a blue "Get started in 3 steps" guide. It will walk you through: (1) Add trucks, (2) Add suppliers, (3) Log your first transaction. Follow these steps in order before doing anything else.

Register every truck in the fleet so transactions can be linked to them

Do this first

Before recording any expense, you must add your trucks here. Every expense must be linked to a vehicle.

Viewing the Vehicle List

Click Vehicles in the left-hand menu. You will see a table listing all registered trucks with their plate numbers, type, make/model, year, status, and monthly budget.

  1. 1
    Click "Add Vehicle" — the blue button in the top-right corner of the page.
  2. 2
    A form opens below the button. Fill in the vehicle details.
  3. 3
    Click "Create Vehicle" to save. The truck appears in the list immediately.
Screenshot: Add Vehicle Form
Shows the white form card with six fields: Plate No, Vehicle Type, Make & Model, Year, Status dropdown, and Monthly Budget

Field Guide

FieldWhat to enter
Plate NoThe vehicle's official plate number (e.g. T 123 ABC). Must be unique — no two vehicles can share the same plate.Required
Vehicle TypeWhat kind of vehicle it is, e.g. "Truck", "Tanker", "Van", "Pickup".Optional

Editing a Vehicle

Click the Edit link next to any vehicle in the table. The same form opens, pre-filled with the current information. Make your changes and click Update Vehicle.

Deactivating a Vehicle

If a truck is no longer in service (sold, written off, or in long-term repair), do not delete it. Instead, edit it and change the Status to Inactive. This preserves all historical expense records linked to that truck.

Cannot delete a vehicle with expenses

If a truck has any expense records, the system will not allow you to delete it. This protects your historical data. Deactivate the vehicle instead.

Searching and Filtering

Use the search bar at the top of the list to quickly find a truck by plate number, type, or make/model. Use the Status dropdown to show only active or inactive trucks.

The core of the system — record every debit and credit for each truck

The Expenses page is where you record every financial transaction — fuel purchases, maintenance costs, insurance payments, and any payments received from suppliers (credits). This is the page you will use most often.

Screenshot: Expenses Page — Table View
Shows the expenses table with filter bar at top, and rows showing date, doc number, vehicle plate, supplier, description, debit/credit amounts, payment status badge, and action buttons

Recording a New Expense

  1. 1
    Click "Add Expense" — the blue button in the top right.
  2. 2
    The form opens. Fill in all required fields (marked with a red asterisk *).
  3. 3
    Click "Create Expense". The entry is saved and a unique Document Number is automatically assigned (e.g. DOC-202604-0001).
Screenshot: Add Expense Form
Shows the expense form with two rows of dropdowns and date pickers at top, a full-width description field, and amount/odometer fields at the bottom

Complete Field Guide

FieldWhat to enter
VehicleSelect the truck this expense belongs to. Only active trucks appear in this list.Required
SupplierSelect the supplier. For example, choose "Said Salim Bakhresa" for a fuel purchase from them. Leave blank if there is no specific supplier.Optional
DateThe date the expense occurred. Defaults to today.Required
Due DateThe date this invoice must be paid by. Used for credit terms (e.g. 30 days after purchase).Optional
Reference NoThe supplier's invoice number or your own reference. This is the number on the paper invoice you receive, e.g. "INV-2026-0045".Optional
TypeCategory of expense: Fuel, Maintenance, Insurance, Parts, or Other.Required
Payment Status • Unpaid — invoice received, not yet paid
• Partial — some payment made, balance remaining
• Paid — fully settled 		Required
Item DescriptionA clear description of what this expense is for. Example: "Diesel 200L — Dar es Salaam to Mombasa trip". Be specific so you can find it later.Required
QuantityHow many units were purchased. For fuel, this is the number of litres. For parts, it could be the number of items.Optional
UnitThe unit of measure. Examples: "Litres", "KG", "Pcs" (pieces).Optional
Debit (TZS)The amount you OWE or SPENT. Enter the total cost of this purchase in Tanzanian Shillings. Do not add commas — just type the number (e.g. 450000).Optional
Credit (TZS)The amount you RECEIVED or PAID BACK. If JIFS made a payment to a supplier, enter it here. This reduces the outstanding balance.Optional
Odometer (KM)The truck's current odometer reading in kilometres at the time of this transaction. Used for calculating cost-per-kilometre in fuel reports.Optional
NotesAny extra information, special instructions, or comments about this entry.Optional

Understanding Debit vs Credit

📤
Debit = Money going OUT

You bought something. You owe the supplier money. Record the cost in the Debit field.
Example: Bought 300L diesel for TZS 450,000 → Debit: 450,000

📥
Credit = Money going IN or paid

JIFS paid a supplier or received a refund. Record it in the Credit field. This reduces what you owe.
Example: Paid supplier TZS 200,000 → Credit: 200,000

Document Number (Auto-Generated)

Every new expense is automatically given a unique Document Number like DOC-202604-0001. This number is assigned by the system — you do not need to type it. When editing an expense, the Document Number is shown in grey (read-only) so it cannot be accidentally changed.

Copying an Existing Expense (Duplicate)

When you need to record the same type of expense for multiple trucks (e.g. fuel for 8 trucks on the same day), use the Copy button instead of filling the form from scratch each time:

  1. 1
    Find the expense row you want to copy and click the grey Copy button.
  2. 2
    The form opens pre-filled with the same vehicle, supplier, type, description, and amount — but with today's date and a blank credit/odometer field.
  3. 3
    Change only what is different (e.g. different truck, different amount) and click Create Expense.
Save time with Copy

If 10 trucks all refuelled at the same station on the same day, record the first one, then use Copy 9 more times and just change the truck plate and amount each time.

Editing an Expense

Click Edit next to any expense to make corrections. Change the fields you need and click Update Expense. The Document Number remains the same.

Deleting an Expense (Admin Only)

Only admin users can delete expenses. Click Del next to the entry and confirm. Deleted expenses cannot be recovered, so be careful.

Searching and Filtering Expenses

Use the filter bar at the top to find specific entries:

  • Search box — Type any word to search across vehicle plate, supplier name, reference number, description, or document number.
  • Type filter — Show only Fuel, Maintenance, Insurance, Parts, or Other expenses.
  • Payment Status filter — Show only Unpaid, Partial, or Paid entries.
  • Date range — Enter a From and To date to see expenses within a specific period.

Click Clear filters to go back to the full list.

CHAPTER 6

Print Statement — Supplier Reconciliation

Generate a ledger statement to compare with what your supplier sends you

Every month, suppliers like Said Salim Bakhresa send JIFS a statement showing all transactions and the outstanding balance. The Print Statement feature generates JIFS's own version of this document so you can compare them side by side and confirm the numbers match. This is called reconciliation.

Screenshot: Print Statement Page — Filter Panel
Shows the filter card with From Date, To Date, and the supplier selection buttons (All Suppliers or individual supplier buttons in blue when selected)

Generating a Statement

  1. 1
    The statement for the current month loads automatically when you open this page. You will see the data immediately.
  2. 2
    To change the period: Update the From Date and To Date fields at the top.
  3. 3
    To filter by supplier: Click one or more supplier buttons. The button turns blue when selected. Click "All Suppliers" to see everyone combined.
  4. 4
    Click "Generate Statement" to reload with your new filters.

Reading the Statement Table

The statement table matches the same column format that suppliers use in their own statements, making comparison easy:

ColumnMeaning
DateThe date of the transaction.
Due DateThe payment due date for this invoice.
Document NoJIFS's internal document reference (auto-generated, e.g. DOC-202604-0001).
ReferenceThe supplier's invoice number entered when recording the expense.
Item DescriptionWhat the expense was for.
Truck NoThe plate number of the vehicle.
QTYQuantity (e.g. litres of fuel).
UOMUnit of measure (e.g. Litres).
DebitMoney owed to the supplier (shown in red).
CreditPayments made to the supplier (shown in green).
BalanceRunning total — the cumulative amount owed after each row. If this turns red, it means JIFS owes money.
Screenshot: Statement Table with Running Balance
Shows the ledger table with all columns, running balance column in dark/red, and the totals footer row at the bottom

Summary Cards

Below the table are three summary boxes: Total Debit (everything owed), Total Credit (everything paid), and Closing Balance (what is still owed). These match the totals row at the bottom of the supplier's statement.

Printing the Statement

  1. 1
    Once the statement is generated, click the blue Print button in the top-right corner.
  2. 2
    Your browser's print dialog opens. Select your printer and click Print.
  3. 3
    The printout will hide the menu and filter panel — only the clean statement with the JIFS logo header is printed.
Save as PDF instead of printing

In the print dialog, change the printer destination to "Save as PDF". This creates a digital copy you can send by email or WhatsApp.

Documents — Vehicle Documents & Licences

Track TRA stickers, road licences, insurance, and all official vehicle documents

Every truck in Tanzania must carry valid official documents. Missing or expired documents can lead to fines, impounding, or being stopped at roadblocks. The Documents page tracks all of these with expiry date alerts so nothing slips through.

Screenshot: Documents List
Shows the documents table with columns: Truck, Document Type, Name/Number, Issue Date, Expiry Date, Status badge (green/amber/red), and Edit/Delete actions. Red alert banner appears at top if any are expired.

Alert Banners

If any documents are expired or expiring within 14 days, red and amber warning banners appear at the top of the Documents page. The Dashboard also shows these alerts in a dedicated "Vehicle Documents" panel.

Status Badges

Each document row shows a coloured badge based on its expiry date:

  • 45 days left — Valid. No immediate action needed.
  • 25 days left — Expiring within 60 days. Plan renewal soon.
  • 3 days left — Critical. Renew immediately.
  • Expired 5d ago — Document has already expired. Urgent action required.

Filtering the List

Use the three filters at the top to narrow down what you see: search by truck plate or document number, filter by document type, or filter by status (Expired, Expiring Soon, or Valid).

Update expiry dates when you renew

When a document is renewed, don't add a new record — edit the existing one and update the Issue Date and Expiry Date to the new dates. This keeps one record per document per truck.

Reports — Financial Analysis

Analyse fleet costs, fuel consumption, supplier balances, and more

The Reports page provides 8 different views of your data. Click any tab across the top to switch between report types. Reports with a date filter will show a From/To date bar; others load automatically.

Screenshot: Reports Page — Tab Navigation
Shows the 8 tab buttons across the top (By Vehicle, By Category, Transactions, Fuel, Supplier Balances, Services, Parts Expiry, Monthly Overview), with the active tab highlighted in blue

Tab 1 — By Vehicle

Shows total spending per truck within a selected date range. Useful for comparing which trucks are costing the most.

  • Transactions — how many expense records exist for that truck
  • Total Debit — total money spent on that truck
  • Total Credit — total payments received/made for that truck
  • Net Cost — Debit minus Credit (the actual net expenditure)

Tab 2 — By Category

Shows how much has been spent in each expense category (Fuel, Maintenance, Insurance, Parts, Other) within a selected period. Helps you understand where the money is going.

Tab 3 — Transactions

A full list of every transaction within a date range, with all details visible: date, truck, supplier, description, reference number, debit, and credit. The footer shows total debits and total credits for the period.

Tab 4 — Fuel

Fuel-specific report showing per-truck statistics for the selected period:

  • Fill-ups — how many times fuel was purchased for this truck
  • Total Litres — total fuel volume in litres
  • Total Cost — total amount spent on fuel
  • Cost per Litre — automatically calculated average price
Screenshot: Fuel Report Tab
Shows the fuel table with per-truck fill-ups, litres, cost, and cost-per-litre columns, with totals row at the bottom

Tab 5 — Supplier Balances

Shows how much JIFS currently owes to each supplier across all time (not limited to a date range). The "Outstanding Balance" column shows the total Debit minus total Credit — if it is greater than zero (shown in amber), JIFS still owes that supplier money.

Compare with supplier statements

Use the Supplier Balances report together with the Print Statement page. Supplier Balances gives you a quick overview of all suppliers; Print Statement gives you the full line-by-line detail for one supplier at a time.

Tab 6 — Services (Maintenance Schedule)

Shows two sections:

  • ● Overdue Services — trucks whose service date has already passed. The column shows how many days ago it was due.
  • ● Upcoming (next 60 days) — trucks with service due soon. Days are colour-coded: green (safe), amber (within 14 days), red (within 7 days).

Tab 7 — Parts Expiry

Similar two-section layout for parts:

  • ● Expired Parts — parts that have already passed their expiry date
  • ● Expiring (next 90 days) — parts expiring soon, colour-coded by urgency

Tab 8 — Monthly Overview

A full breakdown for a single month. Use the Month picker to choose which month to view.

Screenshot: Monthly Overview
Shows 4 summary cards at top (Transactions, Total Debit, Total Credit, vs Last Month % change), then two side-by-side tables: Breakdown by Category and Breakdown by Truck

The four summary cards include a comparison against the previous month — a green percentage means spending went down, red means it went up.

Printing Reports

Once a report is loaded, click the blue Print button in the top-right corner. The sidebar and filter panel are hidden in the printout, leaving a clean table. You can also save it as a PDF from the print dialog.

CHAPTER 11

Daily Workflow — How to Use the System

Step-by-step guide for a typical working day

Morning Routine (5 minutes)

1. Check the Dashboard

  1. 1
    Open the system and log in. The Dashboard loads automatically.
  2. 2
    Check the Budget Warnings card — any truck spending too much will appear here.
  3. 3
    Check Upcoming Services — take note of any trucks due for service this week.
  4. 4
    Check Vehicle Documents — any expired or near-expiry documents will be highlighted in red. Arrange renewals immediately.

Recording Daily Expenses

2. Log Fuel Purchases

  1. 1
    Collect all fuel receipts or delivery notes from drivers.
  2. 2
    Go to Expenses and click Add Expense.
  3. 3
    Select the truck, choose type "Fuel", enter the supplier, description, litres (Quantity), and the cost (Debit). Enter the odometer reading if available.
  4. 4
    For multiple trucks at the same station: record the first truck, then use Copy for each remaining truck and adjust the plate and amount.

3. Record Other Expenses

For maintenance, insurance premiums, parts purchases, or any other costs, follow the same process as fuel — just change the Type and Description accordingly.

4. Record Payments to Suppliers (Credits)

When JIFS makes a payment to a supplier, record it as a new expense entry but put the payment amount in the Credit field (not Debit). Select the same supplier. This reduces the outstanding balance on the supplier statement.

Also update the Payment Status of relevant invoices to Paid or Partial.

End of Month Routine

5. Reconcile with Supplier Statements

  1. 1
    When the supplier sends their monthly statement, go to Print Statement.
  2. 2
    Set the date range to match the supplier's statement period.
  3. 3
    Select the specific supplier using the toggle buttons.
  4. 4
    Click Generate Statement and compare each line with the supplier's document. The totals should match.
  5. 5
    Print or save as PDF for your records.

6. Review Monthly Spending

  1. 1
    Go to Reports → Monthly Overview.
  2. 2
    Select the month you want to review.
  3. 3
    Check the comparison vs previous month. If spending increased significantly, look at the "Breakdown by Category" table to see what changed.
  4. 4
    Look at "Breakdown by Truck" to identify any trucks that are costing unusually more than others.

First-Time Setup (One-Time Process)

Complete setup in this order:

  1. 1
    Add all vehicles — Go to Vehicles and add every truck with its plate number. Set a monthly budget for each if desired.
  2. 2
    Add all suppliers — Go to Suppliers and add every company you buy from.
  3. 3
    Add vehicle documents — Go to Documents and add the TRA sticker, road licence, and insurance for every truck with their expiry dates.
  4. 4
    Start recording expenses — You are ready to begin the daily routine above.

CHAPTER 12

Common Questions & Troubleshooting

Answers to the most common issues users encounter

The system is not opening in the browser

The system must be started first

The JIFS Fleet system needs to be running on the computer before you can open it in the browser. Ask your IT contact or the person who set it up to run the startup script (start-jifs.sh) on the office computer. Once started, open the browser and go to http://localhost:5173.

I cannot find my vehicle in the Expenses form

Only Active vehicles appear in the vehicle selection dropdown. If a truck is not showing up, check that it is registered in the Vehicles page and that its status is set to Active.

I made a mistake in an expense entry

Go to the Expenses page, find the entry, and click Edit. Correct the fields and click Update Expense. If you need to remove the entry entirely, an Admin can click Del to delete it.

The statement balance does not match the supplier's statement

If the closing balance is different from what the supplier shows:

  • Check that all invoices from the supplier have been entered in Expenses.
  • Check that all payments made to the supplier are recorded as Credit entries.
  • Make sure the date range exactly matches the supplier's statement period.
  • Look for any entry where the Debit and Credit amounts may have been swapped.

I cannot delete a vehicle

If you see a message saying the vehicle cannot be deleted, it means there are expenses linked to it. Instead of deleting, edit the vehicle and change its Status to Inactive. This hides it from the expense form but keeps all historical data.

The budget warning is showing incorrectly

The budget warning compares spending for the current calendar month only against the monthly budget set on the vehicle. If the budget figure is wrong, go to Vehicles, click Edit on that truck, and update the Monthly Budget amount.

I forgot my password

Ask your system Admin to reset your account. The Admin can create a new user for you. There is no automatic password reset — only an Admin can manage user accounts.

Data backup

Back up the database regularly

All data is stored in a single file at: backend/data/fleet.db on the office computer. Copy this file to a USB drive or external hard disk at least once per week. If the computer is lost or damaged, this file is the only way to recover your data.
